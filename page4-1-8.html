C(n, d) — Modular Distance Stratification

$$C(n)\;=\;\sum_{d\ge 1} w(d)\cdot C(n,d)$$
C(0, d) = block-coprime density restricted to min(r,M−r) = d ·  C(0) = 1.00000000
The global constant C(n) stratifies by modular distance d = min(r, M−r). Each stratum d has density C(n,d) and weight w(d). The diagonal decomposition connects to the Gap Diagonal Identity — same lattice, perpendicular axis.

Decomposition identity: C(n) = ∑d≥1 w(d)·C(n,d), where w(d) = #{(r,M) : min(r,M−r) = d} / #{all pairs}. At d=1 every cell (1, M) has gcd(1,M)=1 so C(n,1) is near-maximal. As d grows the density falls, governed by φ(d)/d from the Gap Diagonal Identity.
