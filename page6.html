Structure and Derivation
Setup. For integer $s \geq 2$, the probability that $s$ randomly chosen positive integers are mutually coprime equals $1/\zeta(s)$. The block-coprime condition shifts each local Euler factor at prime $p$ from $1 - 1/p^s$ to $1 - \min(n+1,p)/p^s$. Multiplying by $\zeta(s)$ normalizes so that $C_{sD}(0) = 1$.
01Local Factor at Prime p▶

Fix a prime $p$. The s-dimensional block constraint eliminates $\min(n+1,p)$ residue classes mod $p$ from the $p^s$ total. The surviving fraction is $$\ell_p(n,s)=1-\frac{\min(n+1,p)}{p^s}.$$ At saturation $p \leq n+1$: $\ell_p = 1 - p^{1-s}$. Before saturation: $\ell_p = 1-(n+1)/p^s$.

02Normalization by ζ(s)▶

At $n=0$, $\min(1,p)=1$ for all $p$, so $\prod_p(1-1/p^s)=1/\zeta(s)$. Thus $$C_{sD}(0)=\zeta(s)\cdot\frac{1}{\zeta(s)}=1$$ regardless of $s$, giving a uniform baseline across all dimensions.

03Large s Limit▶

As $s\to\infty$, $\zeta(s)\to 1$ and each local factor $\to 1$, so $C_{sD}(n)\to 1$ for all $n$. The dominant correction is the $p=2$ factor, of order $2^{-s}$. In the lattice above (which always shows $s=2$), this manifests as the familiar block-coprime density pattern.

04Recovery of C(n) at s = 2▶

Setting $s=2$ gives $$C_{2D}(n)=\zeta(2)\cdot\prod_p\!\left(1-\frac{\min(n+1,p)}{p^2}\right)=C(n).$$ In particular $C_{2D}(1)=\zeta(2)\cdot D_{FT}\approx 0.5307$, consistent with all results on the C(n) page. The lattice hero always visualizes this $s=2$ case.
